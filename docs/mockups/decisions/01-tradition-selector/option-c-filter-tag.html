Decision 1C: Tradition as Filter/Tag

Content-first approach. The dashboard shows a mixed feed of cards from all traditions, each tagged with a tradition badge. A filter bar at the top lets users show all traditions or narrow to one. Cards appear in chronological/relevance order, creating a discovery-oriented experience.

This treats traditions like tags or categories rather than modes. Users see everything at once and filter down when they want focus. Each card has its own visual identity through a colored left border and badge. This works well for users exploring multiple traditions but can feel chaotic when traditions have very different data shapes (Vedic panchang vs. Chinese BaZi vs. Western aspects).

Pros
  • Modern, discovery-oriented -- shows the breadth of the platform
  • No switching required -- everything visible at once
  • Great for new users exploring what each tradition offers
  • Filter is fast and non-destructive (just hiding, not changing context)
  • Content naturally sorted by relevance/recency
Cons
  • Can feel chaotic with mixed data shapes (panchang + BaZi + aspects)
  • Power users may find it noisy for daily use
  • Harder to build deep workflows within one tradition
  • Feed ordering becomes a complex product decision
  • Less structured than dedicated tradition views
