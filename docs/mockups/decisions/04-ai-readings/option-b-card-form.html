Design Annotation — Option B: Card-Form Feed

Format: Feed-based card stream, similar to a social media timeline or RSS reader. Each reading is a scannable card with tradition badge, type badge, excerpt, and a visual element unique to its type (transit position, dasha progress bar, energy pills, compatibility score).

Best for: Frequent, shorter readings — Transit updates, Dasha period insights, weekly energy forecasts. Content that users consume quickly and repeatedly. The format encourages daily engagement: users check their feed for new insights the way they check news or social media.

Engagement pattern: Users spend 1-2 minutes scanning the feed, then 2-3 minutes on individual cards they click into. Session frequency is higher than long-form (daily vs. weekly). The filter bar enables targeted browsing. "Generate New Reading" CTA drives quota usage and subscription upgrades.

Card types with unique visual elements: Birth Chart cards show key insight previews. Transit cards show planet position and duration. Dasha cards show progress bars with timeline. Weekly forecasts show energy pills (Career/Love/Health). Compatibility cards show the Ashtakoot score with a visual bar. Each type is instantly recognizable at a glance.

Bridge to long-form: Cards are not an alternative to Option A — they are the entry point. "Continue reading" on deep readings (Birth Chart, Compatibility) links to the full editorial page from Option A. The feed is the discovery layer; the editorial is the depth layer. Both formats should coexist.

Trade-offs: Individual cards feel less premium than full editorial readings. Risk of "notification fatigue" if too many readings are generated. Requires careful curation — featured/pinned cards, chronological ordering, and type-based filtering to prevent overwhelm. Cards must be visually distinct enough to not blur together.
