Decision 3C: Chart + Sliding Panel (Tool Mode)

A two-panel layout where the chart dominates the left side (60%) and a sliding detail panel occupies the right (40%). Users click on planet markers in the chart to populate the right panel with that planet's details -- sign, degree, nakshatra, interpretation, aspects, and divisional positions. The chart becomes an interactive tool. When no planet is selected, the right panel shows an overview with key stats.

Pros: Most interactive and professional feel. Keeps the chart always visible while exploring details -- no loss of context. Direct visual-to-data connection (click planet, see its data). Natural exploration flow. Power users can quickly compare planets by clicking through them. Aspect lines can be toggled on the chart itself.
Cons: Most complex to build -- requires an interactive chart with clickable elements. Harder to see a full table of all planets at once. Right panel has limited width for dense data like aspect matrices. Discovery problem: users must know to click things. Not as good for printing/exporting a full report.
Best for: Power users and professional astrologers who want an interactive exploration tool. Users who think visually and want to understand chart relationships spatially. Premium product feel. Users who want to understand "what does this placement mean?"
