Decision 3B: Tabbed Sub-Navigation

Chart data is organized into focused tabs below the chart visualization. The chart itself remains always visible at the top, while the content area below switches between tabs: Overview, Planets, Houses, Aspects, Divisional, Yogas, and Dasha. Each tab presents a focused, digestible view of one data category.

Pros: Organized and scannable. Each tab is focused and digestible -- no overwhelming data walls. The chart remains visible while users explore specific data areas. Easy to add new tabs for future features (transits overlay, AI analysis). Tab names act as a table of contents. Each section loads independently.
Cons: Requires more clicks to see all data. Users cannot easily compare across tabs (e.g., see planets and aspects at the same time). Tab content can feel disconnected from other sections. Not ideal for printing -- each tab would need separate export. Some users may not discover all tabs.
Best for: Users who want focused exploration of specific data areas. Regular users who know what they are looking for. Progressive disclosure -- shows complexity gradually. Mobile-friendly pattern that scales down well.
