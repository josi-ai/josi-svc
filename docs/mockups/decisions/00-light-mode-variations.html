Dark Mode -- The Current Default

Feel

Deep, immersive, celestial. Like looking at the night sky through a telescope. Professional and premium.

Why it works for astrology

Stars are seen at night. The dark backdrop makes gold accents glow like celestial bodies. Charts and data visualizations pop with excellent contrast.

Common in

Discord, Spotify, GitHub (dark), Linear, Raycast, most developer tools and astronomy apps

Current Light Mode

Pure White -- The "Too Bright" One

Feel

Clean and clinical. High contrast. Can feel sterile and harsh, especially on large monitors or in dim rooms. The #FFFFFF cards against #F0F2F7 page lack warmth.

The problem

Pure white is fatiguing for extended use. Astrology is contemplative -- the brightness clashes with the reflective, mystical mood the content aims for.

Common in

Google Workspace, older Material Design, Stripe docs. Many apps are moving away from pure white.

Candidate

Warm Cream -- Parchment & Paper

Feel

Cozy, inviting, like aged parchment or a well-loved book. Less clinical than pure white. The warm undertone feels organic and grounded.

Why it works for astrology

Astrology has roots in ancient texts and handwritten charts. The warm tones evoke that heritage. Gold accents feel natural here -- like candlelight on paper.

Common in

Notion (light), Bear, iA Writer, Kindle, many reading and writing apps. Also common in luxury/wellness brands.

Candidate

Cool Mist -- Refined & Modern

Feel

Professional, calm, slightly ethereal. The blue-gray undertone feels modern and tech-forward without being cold. More subdued than pure white.

Why it works for astrology

The cool tones evoke sky and air -- celestial without being dark. Works well for a platform that also serves as a B2B API. Professional but still mystical.

Common in

Linear, Figma (light), Vercel, many SaaS dashboards. The "enterprise-friendly" light mode.

Candidate

Dark Sidebar + Warm Content -- The Hybrid

Feel

Structured and grounded. The dark sidebar provides a stable anchor while the warm content area is easy on the eyes. Creates a sense of depth and hierarchy.

Why it works for astrology

The dark sidebar feels like the night sky frame, while the content area is where the "reading" happens -- like candlelit pages. The contrast naturally draws focus to content.

Common in

Notion (sidebar stays dark), Slack, Discord, Linear, Obsidian, many pro productivity tools. Very popular hybrid pattern.

Candidate

Dark Sidebar + Cool Content -- Professional Hybrid

Feel

Polished and confident. The high contrast between dark navigation and cool content feels decisive. More "tool" than "experience" compared to the warm hybrid.

Why it works for astrology

Best option if you want Josi to feel like a serious professional tool (for the B2B API audience especially) while still being softer than pure white. The dark sidebar keeps the celestial identity.

Common in

GitHub, GitLab, Jira, Vercel dashboard, Arc browser. The standard "pro SaaS" look.
