Typography — Helvetica Deep Dive & Comparison

Criterion Helvetica (Neue) Inter DM Serif Display + Inter
Origin 1957, Swiss. Max Miedinger. Designed for print signage. 2017, Rasmus Andersson. Designed specifically for screens. DM Serif: 2019, Colophon. Inter: 2017. Both screen-native.
Screen rendering Weak at small sizes. Tight spacing causes readability issues below 14px. "milliliter" is hard to read. Excellent. Tall x-height, open counters. Legible down to 11px. Excellent. Serif headings are large, Inter body handles small sizes.
Dark theme Decent but thin strokes can wash out on dark backgrounds. Designed for screens — renders well on any background. DM Serif's thick strokes shine on dark bg. Inter body is clean.
Personality Intentionally none. "Pursuit of neutrality." Can feel cold/corporate. Warm neutral. Friendly but unremarkable. Serif = ancient wisdom, sans-serif = modern clarity. THE ALCHEMIST.
Brand fit Says "corporation" or "airport signage." Not spiritual, not premium-unique. Safe, modern, but doesn't differentiate Josi from any other SaaS. The font pairing IS the brand story — tradition meets technology.
Licensing Paid. Monotype license required. $$$. Free. Open source. Google Fonts. Both free. Google Fonts. Zero licensing cost.
Cross-platform Not available on all systems. Falls back to Arial (which defeats the purpose). Web font, consistent everywhere. Both web fonts, consistent everywhere.
Used by BMW, American Airlines, Lufthansa, NYC Subway GitHub, Mozilla, most modern SaaS Editorial brands, luxury wellness products
